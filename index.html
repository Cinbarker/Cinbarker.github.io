about

As the printer ink stains on my childhood room rug show, I was taking apart electronics long before I had any clue how they worked. Fast-forward ten years, and I'm working my way through an Electrical Engineering degree, with dozens of projects under my belt. After having gained some experience in both industry and academia, all I know for sure is that this is the field for me.

While growing up people tell you that the powerful politicians, personalities, and wealthy run the world. From trains to telegraphs and now transistors, throughout history the most impactful changes - the paradigm shifts - were always sparked by technological advances. I want to work where the real changes are being made.

I love to learn. I'll never be more comfortable than when taking on a new problem in the safety of my bedroom electronics lab. Equally, I love to teach. My favorite part about teaching electronics, is that as people begin learn more about them, they slowly realize how unfathomably impressive and - for lack of a better word - just plain cool the field is.

My ultimate purpose is to become a professional engineer, and leave innovative technology and knowledge for future generations to inherit, laugh at, and improve upon.
